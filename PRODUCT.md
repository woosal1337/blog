# PRODUCT.md

register: brand

## Product Purpose

Personal site of Ege Çelebi (chele.bi). Two jobs, in this order: a readable blog for longform writing, and a portfolio that presents projects (Clinora, Pulsar, Learnity, open source) on their own surface, separated from the writing. Secondary surface: the bookshelf.

## Users

1. Readers arriving from X or search, landing directly on a post. Often on a phone, often at night. They came to read, nothing else.
2. Founders, collaborators and recruiters skimming who Ege is and what he has built. Desktop, daylight, 30 seconds of attention.

## Brand and Tone

oak.space, one to one, by explicit decision. A solid black terminal, off-white text, 1px hairlines, sharp corners and Geist Mono on everything (tokens recorded in .impeccable/design.json, migrated June 2026 from the previous Apple system). Monochrome, dark only. Voice is direct, no filler, sentence case. Dots and commas only in site copy, no dashes.

This identity commitment overrides generic anti-convergence heuristics. Geist Mono, pure black and the terminal motifs are the brand, not a reflex.

## Strategic Principles

- Readability beats everything. This is a blog first.
- One idea per fold. Generous space between sections.
- Animation is purposeful and rare. Nothing performs.
- Common components everywhere. Every new surface composes existing primitives before inventing new ones.
- Blog posts stay as MDX files exactly where they are. The redesign restyles them, never rewrites them.
- Dark only. There is no light mode and no theme toggle; the canvas is always black.

## Anti-References

- The retro 8-bit aesthetic (dashed borders, pixel fonts, kanji marquee, glitch and scramble effects). Retired.
- Gamified self-presentation (skill bars, lvl badges, quests, guild log). Replaced with plain prose and quiet lists.
- Generic AI portfolio patterns: bento grids, gradient text, dark-with-purple-glow, oversized hover scales.
- The previous Apple-faithful design (white-and-blue, pills, frosted nav, system font). Replaced by the oak terminal system.

## Removed

- The music page, the music library data, and the Spotify now-playing integration. Migrated to YouTube Music; Spotify is no longer supported.

## Kept As Is

- Contribution graph and books shelf: kept, restyled to the terminal system.
